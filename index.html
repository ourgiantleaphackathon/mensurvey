Question #2: How do you think men can help bridge the gender gap in space, science and technology?

Findings:

1. "Men should keep an eye to support their female colleagues - whether it is recognition, or if they may be in a tough uncomfortable situation. Many women get harassed or bullied, and put up with it quietly or end up leaving because they have been sexually harassed or just bullied/limited in their career growth (glass ceiling). Many older women may say "well they had to deal with it before, so they survived it and so should the younger generation" attitude, and may also assume newer generation should 'toughen up' or get out, versus fixing the problem."

2. "Raising awareness and being more aware. Advocating for women's needs if and when they are in a position to do so. Encouraging women and listening to them.

3."I think men who are advocates for gender equality should really own up to their values and beliefs, especially when they are in a group with a majority of men. What I think is a real pitty is sometimes you talk to a man one-to-one, being really supportive of gender-empowerment activities and feeling really angry about sexism in the work environment etc." But then, as soon as you put them in a group of men: they laugh at sexist jokes another colleague will make, when you are victim of sexism they will watch it happen and not defend you, etc. I know it's hard to stand up and raise your voice when faced with peer pressure, but as long as men allies are not able to cross that line - things will not change. If as a man, you are not able to help me out when I am facing sexism and discrimination for being a woman amongst a group - then don't talk to me 1-to-1 and act like you care about my problems and you want to help. I think that is a sad but very important fact to acknowledge."

Question #3: What actions can men take to empower women in the future of space?

Findings:

1."We do not need men to empower us!"

2."Proactively support their women colleagues views. "

3."Be as inclusive as possible for female colleagues (or peers) in opportunities. If you see an all-male panel, raise that issue (especially if you are invited to speak on one, ask who else will be participating and if it will have diverse thought leadership). Many times, women are just simply forgotten to be invited for their technical expertise unless it was about female diversity/gender discussions, and their male colleagues accept offers for speaking without consideration since the organizers may also only know male experts and ask other (usually male) speakers for speaker recommendations."

"4. They can advocate for gender equality in their respective companies - we can create a promotional campaign at SPACE4WOMEN to highlight men in decision-making roles that advocate for gender equality and diversity in #space. also Offer to sponsor and mentor young women through their network - be part of Mentoring S4W program, and In Board rooms/Decision-Making - ensure women are given access and their voice is heard."

Question #4: What could be the best way to promote a gender parity in the future of space?

Findings:

1."There should be "Responsible Inclusive Behavior" guideline for both men/women to learn appropriate professional behavior so women do not feel harrassed or bullied out of the space sector."

2."the main problem I think is lack of candidates in this field. I hope there will be plenty of women engineers in the future. For that, we have to focus on education to get rid of any bias like social role things."

3."Attract girls to STEM at a tender age, nurture their interest in STEM, and promote the professional development of females in space-related activities."

Question #5: Appreciate any other comment and advice to bridge the gender gap

Findings:

1."Space is not only for engineers and scientists. There are varieties of stakeholders now and there are lots of rooms for non-engineers could play a role. The perception on space is special needed to be changed."

2."It is early on that children get raised to be either a "girl" or "boy" - even if the parents try their best to be gender inclusive activities. Therefore, you must change the minds of both the children to be open minded, as well as everyone else around to put stereotypes on women."

3."Men educating and evangelizing other men based on proven results in the market/market needs for diversity and parity is probably the most effective."

Other Initiatives & Long Term Projects

Our Giant Leap

RESEARCH

The RESEARCH team is dedicated to finding the best common practices to embrace diversity and foster gender equality, such as exploring and better defining the role and importance of diversity officers within major companies and organizations of the space sector. We aim to set up working groups with many SGAC partners in order to implement recommendations specifically targeted to their organization types.

  • By working together, we can foster change! This team is led by Yulia Akisheva and Pauline Delande, both also acting as Co-Leads for the whole Diversity and Gender Equality Project Group.

PADAWANS

PADAWANS long-term project aims at introducing to young children and their parents, from all walks of life, a multitude of professions and profiles in the space sector by inviting them to share hands-on experiences with SGAC members in their classrooms. We believe that meeting SGAC volunteers with diverse profiles who work in space will help the children project themselves onto our starry field.

The DIVINAS: DIVersity IN Astronaut Selection long-term project focuses on the diversity in astronaut and other space mission recruitment. Here, we consider whether there is any bias in the selection and training of astronauts

The Communication and Outreach team embodies all communication activities of the Project Group. Work on diversity and inclusion has to be reflected in various forms of communication to reach a wide audience.

FIGURES

The FIGURES: Fill In the Gap in hUman REsearch in Space long-term project aims at contributing to closing the gender data gap in spaceflight research and exploration. How can we send a crew to Mars before we have caught up on the knowledge (and data) about the female body, women psychology and non-binary people in space? Through research and experiments, the FIGURES crew is answering these questions.
